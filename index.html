Problem-Solving & Innovation

Navigating Technical Challenges in ML Deployment

Many projects, including FarmBuddy and NourishKids, were in early-stage development with evolving requirements and limited formal documentation. Rather than waiting for clarity, I took initiative to break down vague objectives into measurable milestones and developed lightweight prototypes to stimulate discussion and guide iteration.

Challenge: Incomplete datasets and API inconsistencies across platforms. Climate modeling had data gaps in temporal consistency and geographic granularity. Sensor data from agricultural cooperatives was sparse and exhibited significant distribution shift across regions.
Solution: Led data preprocessing pipeline development, implementing outlier detection using isolation forests and temporal interpolation for climate time series. Validated data quality through statistical tests and cross-validation with ground truth measurements. For NourishKids, integrated open-source language models and implemented batch processing for efficient resource utilization. Documented all preprocessing steps and assumptions in reproducible Jupyter notebooks.

Systems-Level Recommendations

Working on NourishKids revealed that UI/UX and ML teams often worked on closely related tasks without real-time alignment, causing delays and rework. Beyond organizational processes, I recognized the need for better technical infrastructure:

  • Implemented MLflow for experiment tracking and model versioning
  • Proposed DVC (Data Version Control) for dataset management
  • Established reproducible ML workflows enabling collaboration between teams
  • Created centralized documentation for model architectures and hyperparameters

This systems-level thinking demonstrates my ability to identify both technical challenges and infrastructure gaps that impact ML development workflows, skills directly relevant to distributed ML systems research.

Key Learnings

  • Robust ML under constraints: Real-world deployment requires models that degrade gracefully under data scarcity, handle distribution shift, and maintain calibrated uncertainty, challenges that motivate my interest in optimization theory and robust learning.
  • Offline-first architecture: Designing systems for intermittent connectivity forced me to think deeply about model compression, on-device inference, and asynchronous learning, directly relevant to federated learning research.
  • Human-centered validation: Field deployment revealed that model accuracy alone does not guarantee adoption. Understanding user needs drives better problem formulation and evaluation metrics, an approach I want to deepen through applied research.
  • End-to-end ML systems: Moving from prototype to production exposed challenges in data pipelines, monitoring, and versioning, systems-level thinking that complements algorithmic development.

Reflection & Future Vision

This internship fundamentally shaped how I approach AI system design. Working on FarmBuddy and NourishKids exposed me to the full lifecycle of machine learning systems, from data collection and feature engineering, through model development and optimization, to deployment and monitoring in production environments.

What struck me most was how theoretical ML challenges manifest in practice. Distribution shift is not just an academic concept; it occurs when climate patterns change and crop recommendation models trained on historical data fail. Model uncertainty is not just about confidence intervals; it determines whether a farmer trusts a system enough to change planting decisions. Communication efficiency is not just about bandwidth; it defines whether a federated learning system can train across cooperatives with intermittent connectivity.

These experiences crystallized my research interests: How can we design ML systems that are mathematically robust, computationally efficient, and deployable in resource-constrained environments? How do we optimize for accuracy while maintaining interpretability? How do we handle non-IID data in federated settings?

EPFL's Master of Science in Computer Science, particularly the AI & Data Science specialization, represents the ideal environment to pursue these questions. The program's emphasis on mathematical rigor through courses like Optimization for Machine Learning (CS-439) and Distributed Algorithms (CS-453) will provide the theoretical foundations I need. Research opportunities at MLO, LIONS, and DLAB will allow me to explore federated learning, robust optimization, and multilingual NLP, directly building on the challenges I encountered deploying FarmBuddy and LinguaFusion.

I am sincerely grateful to CRA Group for creating a learning-rich environment and for entrusting me with meaningful work. The technical challenges and insights from this experience will guide my research journey in developing robust, deployable AI systems. I leave this internship with concrete questions, technical skills, and deep motivation to bridge the gap between cutting-edge ML research and real-world deployment.
